Description

Timber Name: Baltic Pine

Botanical Name: Pinus sylvestris

Alternate Name: Scots Pine

Location: Baltic Pine is commonly found in the Northern Hemisphere, especially in Europe and parts of Asia. It is extensively grown in Scandinavia and the Baltic region.

Complete Description:

Baltic Pine features a light, creamy-yellow to pale reddish-brown color. The grain is straight with a medium to coarse texture and prominent knots. It has a density of approximately 510 kg/mÂ³ when dry, making it a moderately dense wood.

Pros:

  • Light and easy to work with.
  • Readily available and cost-effective.
  • Attractive appearance with a warm, rustic look.
  • Good nail and screw holding properties.

Cons:

  • Not as hard as other timbers, making it prone to dents and scratches.
  • Can be susceptible to insect attacks if not treated properly.
  • Requires regular maintenance to keep it in good condition.

Scarcity: Baltic Pine is generally abundant and widely available, making it a popular choice for timber flooring and other woodworking projects.
