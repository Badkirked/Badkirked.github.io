  • Cypress Pine (Callitris glaucophylla) is a durable Australian softwood known for its termite resistance and natural decay resistance, making it ideal for both indoor and outdoor applications.
  • Commonly referred to as White Cypress Pine or Murray Pine, it is found in central western Queensland and New South Wales.
  • The heartwood ranges in color from light yellow through orange to light brown, sometimes with dark brown streaks, while the sapwood is a creamy white.
  • Cypress Pine has a fine, even texture and generally straight grain, often featuring tight knots that enhance its decorative appeal.
  • It is highly durable, with a lifespan of up to 50 years above ground and 25 years in-ground, making it suitable for fencing, decking, and other structural applications.
  • Pre-drilling is recommended for nailing due to its tendency to split, but it grips screws extremely tightly.
  • Cypress Pine is a sustainable choice due to its relatively fast growth and the minimal environmental impact of its harvesting.
  • Primary applications include flooring, cladding, fencing, joinery, and decorative uses such as furniture and turnery.
  • Commonly found in:
    • New South Wales: Central Western regions, including areas around Dubbo and Moree.
    • Queensland: Central Western regions, including areas around Charleville and Longreach.
