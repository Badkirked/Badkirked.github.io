Timber Details

Name: Spotted Gum

Botanical Name: Corymbia maculata, Corymbia citriodora, Corymbia henryi

Alternate Names: Spotted Irongum, Lemon-scented Gum

Location: Grown primarily in New South Wales, Queensland, and Victoria

Description: Spotted Gum is a strong and durable timber with a striking color range from light brown to dark chocolate. It features a wavy grain pattern and a fine texture. This dense wood is commonly used in flooring and is highly regarded for its natural beauty.

Pros:

  • Highly durable and hard-wearing
  • Attractive color variations
  • High resistance to fire, termites, and decay
  • Excellent dimensional stability

Cons:

  • Can be difficult to work with due to its density
  • May require pre-drilling for nails and screws
  • Higher cost compared to some other hardwoods

Scarcity: Readily available in Australia, with sustainable sourcing practices in place.
