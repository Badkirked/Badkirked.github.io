  • Blackbutt is a large Australian hardwood commonly used for structural and exterior applications, as well as for producing plywood.
  • Also known as Coastal Blackbutt or Pink Blackbutt, with the botanical name Eucalyptus pilularis.
  • The name "Blackbutt" came about due to the tree's appearance after bushfire, with the butt significantly darkened.
  • Due to its quick growth and versatility, Blackbutt is a good plantation timber and a commonly available commercial hardwood in New South Wales and southern Queensland.
  • The heartwood ranges from golden yellow to pale brown, sometimes with a slight pinkish color. Sapwood is much paler and resistant to lyctid borer attack.
  • Blackbutt has an even texture and generally straight grain, making it appealing for interior use applications.
  • Can be stained, painted, or polished, but may have issues with painting due to surface checking.
  • High extractives in mature wood can cause problems with adhesives and staining on painted surfaces exposed to weather.
  • Machines well but is only fair for steam bending.
  • Provides good fire resistance and is one of seven hardwood timber species suitable for home construction in bushfire areas in Victoria.
  • Strong and durable, suitable for a range of structural, exterior, and interior applications, including framework, decking, flooring, and poles.
  • Commonly found in:
    • New South Wales: Sydney, Central Coast, Hunter Region (Newcastle, Cessnock, Singleton), North Coast (Coffs Harbour, Port Macquarie, Byron Bay)
    • Queensland: Brisbane, Gold Coast, Sunshine Coast, Maryborough, Fraser Island
  • Primary focus: Timber Flooring
