Timber Details

Name: Tasmanian Oak

Botanical Name: Eucalyptus regnans, Eucalyptus obliqua, Eucalyptus delegatensis

Alternate Names: Victorian Ash, Alpine Ash, Mountain Ash

Location: Grown primarily in Tasmania, Victoria, and New South Wales

Description: Tasmanian Oak is a popular flooring timber known for its light, warm colors ranging from pale cream to pink and reddish-brown. It has a straight grain pattern with a medium to coarse texture. It is moderately dense, making it durable yet easy to work with. This timber is common in Australia and is praised for its versatility and aesthetic appeal.

Pros:

  • Durable and hard-wearing
  • Attractive color range
  • Easy to work with
  • Versatile for various applications

Cons:

  • May be prone to denting
  • Can darken over time with exposure to light
  • Moderate resistance to decay and termites

Scarcity: Readily available in Australia, with sustainable sourcing practices in place.
