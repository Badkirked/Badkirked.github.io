Timber Name: Brushbox

Botanical Name: Lophostemon confertus

Alternate Name: Queensland Brush Box

Location: Brushbox is native to the east coast of Australia, particularly found in Queensland and New South Wales.

Complete Description:

Brushbox timber ranges from pale brown to pinkish brown, often with a hint of orange. The grain is usually interlocked, giving it a wavy appearance, and the texture is fine to medium. It has a density of approximately 900 kg/mÂ³ when dry, making it a very dense and hard wood.

Pros:

  • Very hard and durable, making it resistant to wear and tear.
  • Attractive appearance with rich, warm colors and interlocking grain.
  • Good dimensional stability.
  • Resistant to termites and other insects.

Cons:

  • Can be difficult to work with due to its hardness.
  • Interlocked grain can cause issues during machining.
  • High density makes it heavy and potentially more expensive to transport.

Scarcity: Brushbox is moderately available, with a sustainable supply when sourced from managed forests.
