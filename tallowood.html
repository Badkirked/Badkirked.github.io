Timber Details

Name: Tallowood

Botanical Name: Eucalyptus microcorys

Alternate Names: N/A

Location: Coastal regions from the Hunter River district in New South Wales to Maryborough and Fraser Island in Queensland

Description: Tallowwood is a highly durable timber with heartwood that ranges from pale to dark yellow-brown, sometimes with olive green tinges. It has a distinctive greasy appearance, moderately coarse texture, and generally interlocked grain. This wood is commonly used in flooring due to its durability and attractive appearance.

Pros:

  • Exceptionally durable with a long lifespan both in-ground and above ground.
  • Highly resistant to decay and performs well in damp conditions.

Cons:

  • Sapwood is susceptible to lyctid borer (powder post beetle) attack.
  • May require pre-drilling for nails and screws due to its density.

Scarcity: Tallowwood is generally available in Australia with sustainable sourcing practices in place.
